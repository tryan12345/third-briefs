Personas before pairs 03 / 04

How we find her

We don’t spray product at follower counts. Weeks one and two build two documents from data: the buyer persona from purchase-side signals, the commerce creator persona from seller-side signals. Every archetype gets an Informed Creative Brief that tells a creator exactly what to shoot, which hooks have sold, and which objections to answer on camera. In footwear the big one is sizing. It’s the shade question of shoes, and answering it on camera is a proven conversion format.

Sourcing, gates, and standards · 04 / 04

How the engine runs

Sourcing targets lookalikes of creators already converting premium footwear, managed through our CRM with response, approval, and post rates tracked per campaign. Then the discipline:

  • Seeding gates: 50 to 75 pairs per archetype, day-21 read on GMV per sample and shop-tab trajectory. Scale what clears. Kill what doesn’t.
  • Brand standards at scale: the brief’s non-negotiables and do/don’t lists are how premium positioning survives 400 creators. They are enforceable and measurable.
  • Amplification: winners get usage rights locked, then paid spend. Every top video in every category we pulled this quarter ran paid traffic. Organic performance tells us where to spend.
  • Graduation: Lane 2 creators who convert repeatedly and fit the brand get invited up to Lane 1. The data finds your next co-creation partners for you.

Curation and scale

The roster makes Saucony feel like Saucony. The engine makes the pilot answerable. Both report monthly, on different scorecards, to one strategy.
